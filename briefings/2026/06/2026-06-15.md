## Added to the collection

### A Fixed-Point Neural Operator for Size- and Functional-Transferable Hamiltonian Prediction

HamEvo targets Kohn-Sham Hamiltonian prediction, where the desired Hamiltonian is an implicit fixed point of self-consistent field iteration. Instead of directly regressing the converged Hamiltonian, the method learns a single-step self-consistent update operator, pre-trains it on intermediate self-consistent trajectories, and calibrates the equilibrium with density-matrix supervision.

Why it was added: the central computation is an implicit layer / fixed-point learned operator. The paper reports that iterating HamEvo to convergence improves Hamiltonian prediction across molecular benchmarks, with lower errors than direct regression and deep-equilibrium baselines, transfer to larger molecules with few-shot fine-tuning, and much faster inference than conventional DFT.

Main reader takeaway: this is a strong example of loop-model ideas outside language modeling. The recurrence is not an agent loop or repeated full-model call; it is a learned self-consistent update whose fixed point is the predicted Hamiltonian.
