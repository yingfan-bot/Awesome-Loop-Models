# Daily Loop-Model Watch - 2026-06-29

One high-confidence paper was added today. The useful signal is narrow but strong: it directly studies looped Transformers, where the same Transformer block is applied repeatedly, and asks how to make extrapolation across loop counts less brittle.

## Stabilizing Extrapolation in Looped Transformers via Learned Stochastic Stopping

This paper targets a specific failure mode in looped Transformers. These models can solve variable-length algorithmic tasks by increasing the number of loop applications at inference time, but the paper reports that solutions with good in-distribution accuracy can still show high out-of-distribution variance when sequence length and loop count shift together.

The proposed fix is to treat loop count as a training-time stochastic variable. Randomizing the number of loops during training reduces the shortcut between sequence length and loop count, and the authors also study RL-Halting as a learned stochastic stopping schedule. Across binary addition, Dyck-1, Unique Set, and Copy, learned stochastic stopping often improves the accuracy-stability tradeoff, though it can also stabilize a suboptimal computation.

Why it was added: the architecture is squarely inside the collection boundary. The abstract explicitly says looped Transformers repeatedly apply a shared Transformer block, and the paper analyzes how stochastic loop counts and learned stopping affect that reused-block computation. This is not repeated prompting, agent planning, or a full-model retry loop.

Reader takeaway: "when to stop" is not just a test-time allocation policy for looped Transformers; it changes what computation the recurrent block learns during training.
