# Daily Loop-Model Briefing - 2026-06-04

## Outcome

Three papers were added to the collection. The day adds two June 3 papers that directly extend looped transformer ideas into speech recognition and sparse language modeling, plus one very recent HRM-style compact language-model backfill.

## Added to the collection

### Test-Time Compute Scaling for ASR with Depth-Conditioned Looped Transformers

This paper introduces LARM, a depth-conditioned looped Transformer for automatic speech recognition. Instead of using a fixed-depth acoustic encoder at inference, LARM reuses a shared Transformer block recurrently and makes the number of encoder loops a test-time compute knob. The model structures the loop with sparse CTC checkpoints, supervision-clock embeddings, FiLM depth conditioning, and delayed soft-posterior feedback.

Why it was added: the abstract explicitly centers on reusing a shared Transformer block recurrently inside one acoustic-encoder forward process. It belongs in applications because the looped mechanism is evaluated in end-to-end ASR rather than only as a generic language-model architecture.

Main reader takeaway: the paper reports that increasing the number of inference loops improves LibriSpeech WER and makes the model competitive with deeper unshared-parameter baselines.

### LoopMoE: Unifying Iterative Computation with Mixture-of-Experts for Language Modeling

LoopMoE combines sparse MoE routing with looped, weight-shared computation. Its IterAdaLN module conditions modulation on both the iteration index and per-token hidden state, while a capacity-balancing strategy keeps active parameter ratios comparable to non-looped MoE references. The result is a controlled comparison between looped MoE and vanilla MoE under matched total parameters, per-token FLOPs, and active sublayer ratios.

Why it was added: the paper directly studies looped language models and makes iterative weight-shared computation the central design. It belongs in designs because it proposes a new looped MoE architecture and isolates recurrence under matched compute budgets.

Main reader takeaway: at 3B scale, the abstract reports improvements over matched vanilla MoE on eight of nine downstream benchmarks, with the advantage persisting at 9B scale.

### CosmicFish-HRM: Adaptive Reasoning via Hierarchical Recurrent Mechanisms in Compact Language Models

CosmicFish-HRM explores adaptive reasoning depth in a compact autoregressive language model. Its Hierarchical Reasoning Module cycles through high-level and low-level reasoning states, then learns when to halt based on input complexity. The paper reports non-uniform reasoning behavior across tasks and inputs, with the model allocating different numbers of reasoning steps instead of applying a fixed compute budget everywhere.

Why it was added: the core mechanism is explicitly recurrent and hierarchical inside the model forward process. The paper is not just about repeated prompting or external agent loops; it describes iterative high-level and low-level reasoning cycles inside a learned language-model component with learned halting. It belongs in designs because it proposes and evaluates a concrete architecture.

Main reader takeaway: this is an exploratory, small-scale result, but it is useful as another data point in the HRM-style branch of loop models where adaptive depth is a learned part of the architecture.

## Checked but not added

- **Robust and Efficient Guardrails with Latent Reasoning** (`2605.29068`) transfers multi-step safety reasoning into hidden-state propagation for guardrail classification. The abstract does not make reused learned layer, block, module, or operator recurrence central enough for the strict loop-model scope.
- **Depth-Attention: Cross-Layer Value Mixing for Language Models** (`2606.05014`) improves cross-layer information flow by mixing earlier-layer key-value information inside attention. It is adjacent to depth reuse and the gains extend to looped transformers, but the paper is primarily about cross-layer value retrieval, not a reused internal learned block.
- **Streaming Communication in Multi-Agent Reasoning** (`2606.05158`) studies streaming messages among agents, so its loop is an external multi-agent communication process rather than reused internal model layers.
