# Daily Loop-Model Briefing - 2026-06-24

## Outcome

No paper crossed the strict collection boundary today. The strongest signals were about attractor dynamics, associative-memory steering, long-horizon latent prediction, diffusion cycling, and external verifier loops. Those are adjacent to the language of iterative computation, but none made a learned layer, block, module, or fixed-point operator get reused within one model forward process.

## Checked but not added

- **Reasoning as Attractor Dynamics: Latent Memory Retrieval via Gibbs-Weighted Energy Minimization** (`2606.24543`) models correct reasoning chains as attractor basins and weights sampled reasoning paths by inverse energy. It is adjacent to loop-model inference, but the repeated object is sampled output trajectories, not an internal learned module.
- **Parallel Manifold Steering: Efficient Adaptation of Large Associative Memories via Residual Energy Shaping** (`2606.24396`) learns a state-dependent steering field for frozen Transformer activations. The paper uses attractor and equilibrium language, but the mechanism is adaptation/steering of a single pass rather than recurrence over a reused block.
- **Learning the Koopman Operator using Attention Free Transformers** (`2606.23957`) combines a Koopman autoencoder with an attention-free latent memory block and optional dynamic re-encoding. It is a relevant memory-and-rollout paper, but the loop is temporal prediction plus drift correction, not recurrent-depth reuse in one forward computation.
- **Cyclic Denoising Reveals Ultrastable Memories in Diffusion Models** (`2606.24000`) repeatedly applies forward and reverse diffusion to expose memorized attractors. Diffusion cycling and sampler control are explicitly outside this collection's strict scope.
- **Closing the Loop: Formally Verified Law as a Reward Signal for Self-Improving Legal AI** (`2606.23913`) closes a training/reward loop with an external verifier. That is an agent/training workflow loop, not a loop-model architecture.
