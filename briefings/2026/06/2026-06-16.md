## Added to the collection

### Looped World Models

LoopWM targets the compute-quality tension in world models. Faithful long-horizon simulation often benefits from deeper computation, but conventional deeper models are expensive and can compound errors. LoopWM instead repeatedly applies a shared Transformer block to latent environment states and uses adaptive computation so each prediction step can receive the amount of latent-depth refinement it needs.

Why it was added: the abstract explicitly identifies a same-forward loop over a learned internal module: latent environment states are iteratively refined through a parameter-shared Transformer block. This is not an agent loop, a repeated full-model call, or plain autoregressive decoding.

Main reader takeaway: this is a strong domain expansion for loop models. It moves the recurrent-depth idea from language/reasoning and vision-language-action settings into world simulation, and reports up to 100x parameter efficiency over conventional approaches while treating iterative latent depth as a scaling axis orthogonal to model size and training data.
