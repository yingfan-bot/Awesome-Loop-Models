# Daily Loop-Model Briefing — 2026-06-21

## Outcome

No new paper was added to the main collection today. The newest arXiv batch surfaced useful adjacent work on latent reasoning, diffusion-model transparency, and coding-agent guidance, but none crossed the repository's strict boundary: within one model forward process, a learned layer, block, module, or operator should be reused as the central computation.

## Strongest watchlist signal

### What Makes Effective Supervision in Latent Chain-of-Thought

This paper analyzes why latent Chain-of-Thought training can drift when only outcome supervision is available. It separates supervision into trajectory signals and latent-space structure, then introduces a probe for measuring how much information latent trajectories retain about explicit reasoning steps.

Why it was not added: the paper is about supervising and measuring latent reasoning traces. The abstract does not identify a looped, weight-tied, recurrent-depth, or fixed-point architecture that reuses a learned internal operator within a single forward process. It is good context for latent reasoning evaluation, not a conservative loop-model intake.

## Other near-misses

### How Transparent is DiffusionGemma?

DiffusionGemma is relevant because it studies reasoning transparency when computation happens across denoising steps in continuous latent space. The paper asks whether intermediate denoising states can be made interpretable and reports that token-bottleneck mappings can reduce opaque serial depth without hurting downstream performance.

Why it was not added: the loop is diffusion denoising, where the model changes token predictions over sampling steps. The repo explicitly excludes diffusion or energy-style iterative inference unless the paper's central contribution is a reused learned internal loop-model operator.

### Probe-and-Refine Tuning of Repository Guidance for Coding Agents

This paper tunes repository guidance files by running synthetic bug-fix probes and patching the guidance from the observed failures. It is practical context for agent workflows and instruction quality.

Why it was not added: the refinement happens outside the model as a coding-agent or prompt-guidance workflow. It is not a model architecture with same-forward reuse of a learned layer, block, module, or operator.
