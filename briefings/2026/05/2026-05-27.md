# Daily Loop-Model Briefing — 2026-05-27

## Outcome

Four papers were added to the collection. They cover a tight cluster around looped transformers and looped language models: how to retrofit recurrence onto frozen transformers, how selective transformer-layer looping behaves in masked diffusion language models, how to stabilize recurrent latent dynamics for test-time scaling, and how to convert pretrained LLMs into looped latent refinement systems.

## Added to the collection

### Training-Free Looped Transformers

This paper asks whether a pretrained transformer can be made looped without continued training. The answer is a lightweight inference wrapper that repeatedly applies a contiguous mid-stack block of layers in a frozen checkpoint. Naively reapplying blocks can hurt, so the method treats pre-norm transformer blocks like forward-Euler steps and turns one large update into smaller damped refinement sub-steps.

Why it was added: the loop is an explicit reuse of learned transformer layers inside one inference process. It is a design paper because the contribution is an inference-time architecture wrapper for test-time compute scaling.

Main reader takeaway: the authors report gains across dense, sparse MoE, and MLA+MoE model families, including improvements on MMLU-Pro, CommonsenseQA, and OpenBookQA, without fine-tuning or architectural retraining.

### Looped Diffusion Language Models

LoopMDM brings selective layer looping into masked diffusion language models. The paper loops early-middle transformer layers during training to get a depth-scaling effect without adding parameters, then varies the number of loops at inference time for flexible compute scaling.

Why it was added: diffusion-style iterative generation alone would not be enough for this repository, but here the central mechanism is explicit reuse of internal transformer layers. The paper belongs in architecture and algorithm designs because it proposes a concrete looped model variant.

Main reader takeaway: the abstract reports that LoopMDM can match same-size masked diffusion language models with up to 3.3 fewer training FLOPs, improve reasoning benchmarks by up to 8.5 points on GSM8K, and outperform deeper non-looped MDMs trained with comparable per-step compute.

### Stabilizing Recurrent Dynamics for Test-Time Scalable Latent Reasoning in Looped Language Models

This paper studies a failure mode of Looped Language Models: adding more recurrence at test time can improve performance up to a point, then collapse. The authors analyze latent dynamics and frame useful recurrence as convergence toward stable fixed points while preserving task effectiveness.

Why it was added: the subject is directly the stability of depth recurrence in LoopLMs. It is categorized as analysis because the main contribution is understanding and regularizing recurrent dynamics, although the STARS training framework is also practically useful.

Main reader takeaway: STARS uses Jacobian spectral-radius regularization with random loop sampling to make recurrence more stable. The abstract reports reliable test-time scaling on arithmetic tasks and less performance degradation at higher recurrence depths on complex mathematical reasoning.

### LoopUS: Recasting Pretrained LLMs into Looped Latent Refinement Models

LoopUS converts a standard pretrained LLM into an encoder, a looped reasoning block, and a decoder. It uses staged block decomposition, an input-dependent selective gate, random deep supervision, and a confidence head for adaptive early exit so latent recurrence can be learned without training a recurrent model from scratch.

Why it was added: a learned reasoning block is reused as a latent refinement loop, and that reuse is the central architectural move. It is a design paper because it gives a post-training recipe for turning an existing non-looped LLM into a looped model.

Main reader takeaway: the method targets reasoning-oriented gains from stable latent looping without extending generated traces or requiring full recurrent pretraining.

## Checked but not added

- **Latent Recurrent Transformer** (`2605.26797`) is adjacent but not included. Its recurrence stores state across token positions while preserving standard depth and KV-cache behavior, so it is closer to sequence-time recurrence than same-forward learned-block reuse.
- **Reverse Martingale Recurrent Neural Network for Precipitation Forecasting** (`2605.26401`) is a recurrent forecasting model, but ordinary sequence recurrence is outside the repository's strict loop-model scope.
